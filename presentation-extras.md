## 1. AUDIENCE VOLUNTEER PROTOCOL (Live Hero #1 Demo)

### The Concept
Replace the public-figure demo of Hero #1 with a live audience volunteer's real listing appointment or real address. The room watches a dossier generate on someone they know, in real time. This is the single highest-impact change you can make to the talk.

### When to Use It
- **In-person events only.** Do not attempt this on a webinar -- the lag and screen-share issues will kill the moment.
- **Only if you've rehearsed the backup path.** If the live volunteer demo fails, you must be able to cut to the public-figure backup (Obama Kenwood home) instantly.
- **Only if you have reliable internet.** See the rehearsal checklist for connectivity requirements.

### Pre-Qualification (During the Avatar Fork, Slide 5)
After the avatar descriptions, add this:

**SAY:** "One more thing before we start. I'm going to need a volunteer in about five minutes. Here's what I need: someone in this room who has a listing appointment coming up in the next two weeks. Real appointment, real address, real seller. I'm going to run one of these queries live on YOUR listing appointment in front of the whole room. You'll walk out of here with a one-page dossier on your seller that would have taken you four hours to research manually. Who's got one?"

*(Hands go up. Pick someone in the first three rows -- you need to be able to hear them and they need to be able to see the screen. Prefer someone who looks enthusiastic, not terrified.)*

**SAY:** "What's your name? ... [Name], what's the address? ... And the seller's name? ... Perfect. We're going to come back to you in about five minutes. Don't go anywhere."

*(Write the name and address on a sticky note or type it into a Notes app on the presentation laptop. Do NOT type it into Claude yet -- that comes during the demo.)*

### Consent Script (30 seconds, delivered naturally)
**SAY:** "[Name], two quick things. One -- everything I'm about to pull is from public records and public websites. Nothing private. Two -- are you comfortable with the room seeing the output? Because whatever Claude finds, the room sees it. If there's anything about this seller that you'd rather keep private, tell me now and I'll use a different address. No judgment."

*(Wait for explicit yes. If they hesitate, say "No worries, we'll use a different one" and switch to the public-figure backup. Never pressure.)*

### The Demo (replaces Slide 8 demo break)
1. Cmd+Tab to the pre-loaded Hero #1 Claude tab.
2. Type the volunteer's name and address into the query input.
3. **SAY:** "[Name], watch this. Everyone, watch this."
4. Press Enter.
5. Narrate during generation: "Claude is pulling county records, LinkedIn, news, social media, building permits -- everything public. In about 90 seconds, [Name] is going to have a one-page briefing on a seller they haven't met yet."
6. When output lands, scroll slowly. Read the HEADLINE INSIGHT out loud.
7. Read one RAPPORT HOOK out loud. Turn to the volunteer: "[Name], did you know that about your seller?" (The answer is almost always no.)
8. Read THE ONE QUESTION out loud. Turn to the volunteer: "Would you ask that question in the first 10 minutes?" (The answer is almost always yes.)
9. **SAY:** "That took 90 seconds. That's your edge. [Name], you just walked into your listing appointment better prepared than every other agent competing for it. And you didn't even leave your seat."

### Failure Protocol
- **Claude returns a thin dossier (not enough public info on the seller):** "And this is what happens when the seller has a thin public footprint. Notice Claude told us what it found and what it didn't. The rapport hooks and objections are still useful. But here's the move -- [Name], I'd run this query again tonight with the seller's LinkedIn URL pasted in as additional context, and the dossier will fill out."
- **Claude returns something awkward or sensitive (a bankruptcy, a lawsuit, a messy divorce):** Read the HEADLINE INSIGHT only, skip the sensitive detail, and say: "[Name], I'll send you the full output after the talk so you can review it privately." Do NOT read sensitive information about a real person to a room of 200 strangers.
- **Claude hallucinates (wrong person, wrong address):** "And this is why the VERIFIED/INFERRED tags matter. See this one -- it says INFERRED. That means Claude is guessing. You never repeat an INFERRED fact to a seller as if it's truth. That's the guardrail."
- **Total failure (Claude hangs, internet dies):** Cut to the backup slide with the Obama dossier screenshot. "Let me show you what this looks like when it runs on a more well-known address." Nobody in the room will blame you for switching.

### Risk Assessment
- **Privacy risk:** LOW. Everything in the dossier is from public records and public web. The consent script covers you.
- **Sensitivity risk:** MEDIUM. You cannot control what Claude finds. The "skip sensitive detail" rule is critical.
- **Failure risk:** MEDIUM. Claude might return a thin result if the seller has a minimal public footprint. The failure script handles this gracefully.
- **Drama payoff:** VERY HIGH. This is the moment the room goes silent, then erupts. It's the moment that gets talked about at dinner.

### Recommendation
Run the public-figure backup (Obama) as your PRIMARY rehearsed demo. Treat the audience volunteer as an OPTIONAL upgrade that you offer only if conditions are right (reliable internet, enthusiastic volunteer, in-person venue). If it works, it's the talk of the conference. If conditions aren't right, skip it and nobody knows what they missed.

## 4. AUDIENCE RESULTS MOMENT (insert at minute ~40, before the close)

### Where It Goes
After Hero #5 demo, before the Tool Reality Check slide. Insert as a 60-second unscripted moment.

### Speaker Notes Addition

**SAY:** "Before I wrap up -- I want to try something. Did anyone in this room actually run one of these queries during the talk? On your phone, on your laptop, right now? Raise your hand if you tried one."

"Which one did you run? ... [Let them answer.] ... What did you get? ... [Let them share one result -- the top-ranked contact name, or a rapport hook, or a ghost-note text.] ... That took you how long? ... [They'll say 2-3 minutes.] ... And you did it while sitting in a conference room listening to me talk. That's the whole point. This is not a someday tool. This is a right-now tool."

*(If someone shares a surprisingly good result, react genuinely. If someone says "it didn't work," ask what went wrong -- it's a teaching moment either way.)*

**DO:** This moment cannot be rehearsed. It's either there or it isn't. Do not force it. If no hands go up, move on. If hands go up, let the room do the work -- you become the moderator for 60 seconds, not the presenter. The social proof of a peer saying "I just got a call list in 90 seconds" is worth more than anything you can say.

## 7. Q&A OBJECTION PREP (5 questions you'll get every time)

### Q1: "Isn't this creepy? Especially the LLC unmasking and the dossier?"

**ANSWER:** "Fair question. Everything in these queries uses publicly available information -- county tax records, Secretary of State filings, LinkedIn, news articles. This is the same information a title company pulls, a loan officer reviews, or a good agent researches manually before a listing appointment. The only difference is speed. What used to take four hours now takes four minutes. The query doesn't access anything private. And notice the VERIFIED/INFERRED tags -- the query is designed to tell you what it found versus what it's guessing, so you never repeat a guess as a fact. That's the guardrail.

The LLC unmasking specifically -- these are professional investors. They register business entities specifically to do business. They want to be found by good agents. You are not stalking a private person. You are identifying a business owner."

### Q2: "What about Fair Housing? Can I get in trouble using AI to target certain groups?"

**ANSWER:** "Great question and I'm glad you asked it. Fair Housing applies to your MARKETING and your SERVICE decisions -- who you advertise to, who you agree to represent, how you treat clients. It does not prohibit you from researching a potential client using public information before you reach out to them. Every query I showed you today is about research and preparation, not about selecting or excluding people based on protected classes.

That said -- if you're using AI to build outreach lists, do not ask the AI to filter by race, religion, national origin, familial status, sex, disability, or any other protected class. The queries I showed you filter by behavior signals -- years in home, equity position, life events. Those are not protected classes. Stay on that side of the line and you're fine."

### Q3: "Does this actually work or is the AI just making stuff up?"

**ANSWER:** "Both, and the query is designed to tell you which is which. Every query I showed you has a VERIFIED/INFERRED tagging system. When Claude finds a fact in a public record, it tags it VERIFIED and gives you the source. When it's reasoning or guessing, it tags it INFERRED and tells you why. Your job is simple: never repeat an INFERRED fact to a client as if it were truth.

Is the AI perfect? No. It will occasionally get something wrong. But here's the comparison: the alternative is not researching the client at all, which is what 95% of agents do right now. A dossier that's 85% accurate and tells you which 15% to verify is dramatically better than walking into a listing appointment cold."

### Q4: "I'm not technical. Can I really do this?"

**ANSWER:** "If you can copy and paste, you can do this. That's literally the workflow. Go to tapthis.co/prospecting. Click the copy button on any query. Open Claude -- it's a website, claude.ai, twenty dollars a month. Paste the query. Fill in your specific details -- the address, the name, the CSV. Press enter. Read the output.

You do not need to write the query. I already wrote it. You do not need to understand how AI works. You need to understand how to paste. The queries on the companion page are copy-paste ready. The hardest part is exporting your CRM to a CSV, and I gave you instructions for that too."

### Q5: "Which tool should I buy? Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Grok?"

**ANSWER:** "If you don't have any of them yet, start with Claude Pro. Twenty dollars a month. It's what I use every day and it's what I demoed today. It handles long documents well, it's good at research, and it's good at empathetic writing -- which matters when you're drafting outreach to real humans.

If you already have ChatGPT Plus and you like it, keep it. Every query I showed you today works in ChatGPT too -- you might need to adjust the phrasing slightly, but the structure is the same. Don't switch tools for the sake of switching.

The honest answer is: the best tool is the one you'll actually open every morning. Pick one. Get good at it. You don't need four subscriptions. You need one habit."
