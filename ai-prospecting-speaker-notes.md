### Critical pre-talk checklist

1. **Hero #1 demo target.** Pick a celebrity home sale that closed 3+ years ago, fully documented in press. Do a full dry run of the query in Claude the morning of the talk to confirm the dossier still generates. Screenshot the output as a backup slide.

2. **Hero #2 demo CSV.** Use the synthetic 50-row CSV (`demo-csv-triage.csv`, sibling file). Do NOT use real client data, even fake-named. One real name slipping through is a headline.

3. **Hero #3 ghost notes.** Pre-write three fictional ghost notes into Claude. Rehearse the output at least 3 times. Screenshot backups.

4. **Hero #4 — THE BIG ONE. Read this twice.**
   The LLC unmask demo is the highest-risk live element of the talk. Naming a real third-party investor on a conference screen without their explicit written consent is a headline risk — even if the information is technically public — and it's not worth the extra 10 seconds of drama. **Use one of these three safe approaches instead:**

   **Option A (strongly recommended): Pre-register a teaching LLC.** Three to four weeks before the talk, file a new LLC with the Illinois Secretary of State in your own name, with an obviously pedagogical name like "Avondale Teaching Holdings LLC." Cost is roughly $150 plus filing fees. The trace is 100% real, 100% safe, 100% rehearsable. You get the drama of watching Claude pull live Secretary of State records without exposing anyone else. Bonus: you can tell the room exactly what you did and why, which is a teaching moment in itself.

   **Option B: Your own existing LLC.** If you already have an LLC tied to your own real estate activity, unmask it on stage. Same effect, no filing fees.

   **Option C: Consented colleague LLC.** If a known Chicago investor friend wants the stage time, get explicit written consent and unmask theirs. Turn it into a joint moment — "I called my friend [name] this week and asked if I could do this live. Here's what happens." Get the consent in writing (email reply is fine) and keep it on file.

   **Do NOT** unmask a third-party investor without consent, even if their information is already Google-able. The stage changes everything.

5. **Hero #5 demo.** Run Phase 1 of the GEO query on your own niche the morning of the talk to see what Claude currently says. You need to know in advance whether you're named — the delivery changes. If you are named, the line is "and I don't say this to brag, I say this to show you it's repeatable." If you're not, the line is "and look — I'm not in this list. Yet. And that's the whole point of this query."

6. **Rehearse every demo 5 times minimum.** Every. Demo. Five. Times. Including the exact paste, the exact enter press, the exact scroll motion on the output. Muscle memory is the only thing that saves you when the stage clock gets tight.

7. **Backup screenshots on the slide immediately after each demo.** If Claude hangs or the network dies, click forward and you're still running the talk. Rehearse the backup-slide path too.

8. **Wi-Fi.** Confirm with the venue's AV team that you will have a reliable hardwired connection, not the guest Wi-Fi. If only guest Wi-Fi is available, tether to your own phone hotspot as a backup. Test from the actual stage position before doors open.

### Speaker energy arc

The talk is deliberately shaped as a four-beat arc:

- **Minutes 0–6 (Open):** Provocation and reframe. Low on slides, high on delivery. Establish the thesis and plant the 82% stat.
- **Minutes 6–21 (Heroes 1–3):** Build trust through concrete, practical, "I could do this Monday" wins. These are the warmest, most relatable demos — sphere-focused, working with what they already have.
- **Minutes 21–27 (Hero 4):** Drama spike. LLC unmask is the "holy shit, this is possible?" moment. This is where the talk earns the right to the closer.
- **Minutes 27–35 (Hero 5):** The pivot. Stop asking how to use AI to find clients. Start asking how to make AI find *you*. Callback to the 82% stat. This is also the CTA bridge.
- **Minutes 35–45 (Close):** Tool slide, Monday checklist, companion page, CTA, Q&A. Quick, clean, confident.

### Things that will tempt you to do that you should not do

- **Don't over-explain what Claude is.** If you spend 3 minutes on "here's how LLMs work," you lose Victor and you confuse Marcus simultaneously. Skip it. The demos teach it.
- **Don't apologize for the queries being long.** Length is the feature. Short prompts get short answers.
- **Don't promise deals.** Promise conversations. "This will lead to better conversations" is defensible. "This will 10x your GCI" is Tony Robbins cosplay and the room will feel it.
- **Don't demo more than 5 queries.** The instinct will be to squeeze in a sixth. Don't. Five is the number. The swipe file handles everything else.
- **Don't break the structure of the 5 queries mid-talk.** If you catch yourself saying "actually, let me jump ahead" — don't. Trust the arc.
