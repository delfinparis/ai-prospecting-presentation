# Stage Rehearsal Checklist — Find Your Next Client With AI

**Rule of the stage:** if you haven't run a demo 5 times successfully in the exact conditions you'll run it on stage, it will fail. Muscle memory is the only thing that saves you when the clock is tight and the room is watching.

Do every step on this list. Do not skip. Do not assume. The worst moment in a live AI demo isn't a failure — it's the 20 seconds of dead silence before you realize you need to recover.

---

## T-Minus 4 Weeks

- [ ] Confirm talk date, venue, stage tech (screen resolution, projector aspect ratio, clicker model, mic type, audio for video playback if any)
- [ ] Confirm venue internet: hardwired Ethernet available? Guest Wi-Fi only? Cell signal?
- [ ] Upgrade/confirm Claude Pro subscription is active on the exact machine you will bring on stage
- [ ] Verify `DJP3 Consulting Inc` appears in the Illinois SOS business entity portal when you search it manually — screenshot and save
- [ ] Pick the Hero #1 public-figure target (celebrity home sale, closed 3+ years ago, press-documented). Document the name, address, and 3 press links for the pre-demo brief

## T-Minus 3 Weeks

- [ ] Dry-run all 5 hero queries against their intended inputs, end to end, on the exact machine you will bring. Record video of each for later comparison.
- [ ] Take backup screenshots of every successful output. Drop each into the slide that follows the demo as a hidden backup.
- [ ] Test the tapthis.co/prospecting page (build if not live yet) on mobile, desktop, and tablet. Test the QR code from 15/25/40 feet.
- [ ] Draft the "I couldn't find the human" fallback script for Hero #4 in case Claude doesn't surface your name cleanly on a run.

## T-Minus 2 Weeks

- [ ] Full run-through of all 5 demos, back-to-back, with a timer, as if on stage. Target: under 15 minutes for the 5 demos combined (the 25-min demo budget accounts for narration and transitions).
- [ ] Record the full run. Watch it back. Note every moment you hesitated, narrated into dead air, or scrolled for too long.
- [ ] Second run-through. Fix the things you noticed.

## T-Minus 1 Week

- [ ] Full rehearsal with slides, in front of one person (a trusted partner — podcast co-host, team member, spouse). Get feedback on pacing.
- [ ] Confirm all 5 demos STILL work (Claude sometimes changes behavior week to week — test, don't assume).
- [ ] Print one paper copy of the speaker notes and all 5 query texts. You will not use them on stage, but the printed copy is your failsafe if the slide deck itself fails.
- [ ] Charge a backup laptop with Claude Pro logged in, ready as a hot swap.
- [ ] Confirm phone hotspot works from the venue region; have it tested and named.

## T-Minus 24 Hours

- [ ] Full run-through on the actual travel laptop, on the hotel or home Wi-Fi, as a final check.
- [ ] Screenshots of every successful output saved to the slide deck as backup slides.
- [ ] Clear Claude's conversation history for any conversation windows you'll be demoing from — no stale context.
- [ ] Open 5 fresh Claude conversation windows, one per hero query, with each query already pasted (and the input ready to paste). Label each window tab with the hero number.
- [ ] Charge everything. Bring chargers.

## T-Minus 1 Hour (at the venue)

- [ ] Test the stage laptop's connection on the venue's actual network from the actual stage position
- [ ] Open all 5 Claude tabs, confirm each query is loaded
- [ ] Open the slide deck in presentation mode, click through from slide 1 to end, confirm every animation and backup screenshot renders
- [ ] Test the QR code on slide 27 by scanning it from the back row
- [ ] Mic check, clicker check, water bottle placed
- [ ] Run Hero #1 query one more time in a fresh tab to confirm it still generates (this is your "is the internet alive" smoke test)

## DEMO 1 — PRE-LISTING INTELLIGENCE DOSSIER

**Hero slide:** 6–9 | **Demo slide:** 8 | **Budget:** ~150 seconds on screen, ~60 seconds of narration over it

### Pre-load (before talk starts)
1. Open a fresh Claude conversation, tab-label "HERO 1 — DOSSIER"
2. Paste the full Hero #1 query at the top of the conversation
3. Below the query, paste the public-figure target: full name + full address + "the appointment is tomorrow morning at 10am"
4. **Do not press enter.** The input is staged and ready.

### On-stage paste/click sequence
1. Click to slide 8. Cmd+Tab to Claude.
2. Pause, look at the audience, deliver the narration line: *"I've got the query ready. I'm pasting in a name and an address…"*
3. Press **Enter**.
4. While Claude is thinking (~20–40 sec): deliver the mid-run narration line *"Notice what it's already doing — it's searching, it's tagging…"*
5. Once the dossier lands, scroll **slowly** from top to bottom. Do not click anything.
6. Read the HEADLINE INSIGHT out loud, verbatim.
7. Read one of the RAPPORT HOOKS out loud, verbatim.
8. Cmd+Tab back to slide 9.

### Success markers (how you know it's working)
- Claude produces a section labeled HEADLINE INSIGHT
- At least 3 VERIFIED tags and at least 2 INFERRED tags appear
- The PRICING ANCHOR paragraph is present
- The table of top 3 objections renders with pre-handles
- Sources section at the bottom has at least 4 linked sources

### Failure modes and recovery
- **Claude hangs for >20 seconds with no output streaming:** Say *"And while this one is thinking, let me show you a run I did last night on a different property."* Cmd+Tab to the backup screenshot on slide 8a. Walk through the backup like it's the live demo. Nobody will know.
- **Claude returns a dossier but a key section is missing:** Say *"Notice what it got and what it didn't — and this is a good teaching moment. The VERIFIED/INFERRED tagging is what tells you when to trust it."* Lean into it. Don't apologize.
- **Wi-Fi dies:** Cmd+Tab to backup screenshot. Continue as if planned.
- **The public figure target turns out to be thinly documented:** You will catch this in the T-3 weeks dry run. Swap the target before stage day.

### Rehearsal count
**Minimum 5 successful runs** on the exact machine and network you'll use on stage. Record at least one on video.

## DEMO 2 — DATABASE TRIAGE

**Hero slide:** 10–13 | **Demo slide:** 12 | **Budget:** ~180 seconds on screen

### Pre-load
1. Open a fresh Claude conversation, tab-label "HERO 2 — TRIAGE"
2. Paste the full Hero #2 query at the top
3. **Upload** `/Users/djparis/demo-csv-triage.csv` as an attachment (drag into the conversation). Do not paste the CSV as text — the upload is the demo.
4. **Do not press enter.** The upload is attached, the query is written, the send button is armed.

### On-stage paste/click sequence
1. Cmd+Tab to Claude.
2. Deliver the setup line: *"I've got a sample database loaded — 50 contacts, fake names, real-looking notes."*
3. Press **Enter**.
4. While Claude runs (~30–50 sec): fill with *"Watch the output. Top 10, ranked, scored…"*
5. When the output lands, scroll slowly to the top ranked contact.
6. Read the #1 contact's name, the quoted note, and the opening line **verbatim** from Claude's output.
7. Pause for a beat. Let the room process it.
8. Scroll down to the "nurture only" section. Read the header.
9. Scroll further to the "dead weight" count. Read the number.
10. Cmd+Tab back to slide 13.

### Success markers
- Output is formatted as three tables
- Top 10 table has all columns: Rank, Name, Score, Strongest Signal, Quoted Note, Opening Line
- The top-ranked contact should be one of: Jessica Okafor, Hannah Weiss-Cooper, Nadia Petrov-Sanchez, Priscilla Oduya, or Regina Adetola (all seeded as high-score candidates)
- The opening line contains a quoted specific from the notes (not a generic template)

### Failure modes and recovery
- **Claude ranks someone unexpected at #1:** Good — the demo still works. Read whatever Claude ranked first. The point is the structure, not the specific name.
- **Opening line sounds generic:** Very unlikely given the prompt's constraints, but if it happens: *"Notice it's not perfect — you'd tune this in your own voice. But the raw material is here and it's specific."*
- **CSV upload fails:** Backup plan — have the query pre-run with output screenshotted on slide 12a. Switch to it.
- **Claude returns only 5 contacts instead of 10:** Say *"And it gave me the top 5 — let me run a follow-up: 'continue the list to 10.'"* Type that, hit enter, wait for the rest.

### Rehearsal count
**Minimum 5 successful runs.** Seed yourself with what the expected top 10 looks like so you're not surprised on stage.

## DEMO 3 — GHOST NOTES REACTIVATION

**Hero slide:** 14–17 | **Demo slide:** 16 | **Budget:** ~150 seconds on screen

### Pre-load
1. Open a fresh Claude conversation, tab-label "HERO 3 — GHOST NOTES"
2. Paste the full Hero #3 query
3. Below the query, paste three fictional ghost notes. Suggested:
   ```
   Brianna + Mark — met at OH Lincoln Park summer 2022, pregnant with first (girl), budget 600, husband software eng at Groupon, wanted "a yard for the dog"

   Kevin O'Neill — came to OH Wicker Park 2022, said he + fiancée were waiting to get married before buying, budget 500, she's a nurse at Northwestern, he's a bartender

   Amira Jafari — walk-in OH Bucktown late 2022, investor-curious, mentioned her dad owned rentals in Rogers Park, said "I want to buy my first investment in the next year or two"
   ```
4. **Do not press enter.**

### On-stage paste/click sequence
1. Cmd+Tab to Claude.
2. Deliver the line: *"Three ghost notes. All made up, all realistic. Let's watch it work."*
3. Press **Enter**.
4. While it runs (~40 sec): *"Look at what it's doing — reconstructing, projecting forward, and then writing the four touches."*
5. When output lands, scroll to the first lead's output.
6. Read the RECONSTRUCT paragraph silently to yourself (let audience read it). Then read the AGE-FORWARD paragraph **out loud.**
7. Read Touch 1 (the text message) out loud, verbatim. Let it land with a beat — this is the moment the room realizes the output sounds like a human.
8. Scroll to the summary table at the top. Read one confidence rating.
9. Cmd+Tab back to slide 17.

### Success markers
- Summary table appears BEFORE the per-lead sequences
- Every lead has RECONSTRUCT, AGE-FORWARD, and 4 labeled touches
- No Touch contains the phrase "just checking in," "crazy market," or "interest rates"
- No Touch contains an apology for the time gap
- Text messages are 2 sentences or fewer
- Handwritten card messages are 4 sentences or fewer

### Failure modes and recovery
- **A touch contains a forbidden phrase:** Extremely rare given the constraint wall in the prompt, but if it happens: *"See how it almost wrote 'just checking in' there? That's why the constraints matter — this is the edit you'd make before sending."* Play it as a teaching moment.
- **Claude skips a lead:** Say *"Two out of three — and in real life you'd just re-run."*
- **Output is too short/generic:** Unlikely with 3 seeded detailed notes. If it happens, cut to backup screenshot.

### Rehearsal count
**Minimum 5 successful runs.**

## DEMO 4 — BUSINESS ENTITY UNMASKING (DJP3 CONSULTING INC)

**Hero slide:** 18–21 | **Demo slide:** 20 | **Budget:** ~180 seconds on screen | **HIGHEST RISK DEMO**

### Pre-load
1. Open a fresh Claude conversation, tab-label "HERO 4 — UNMASK"
2. Paste the full Hero #4 query (the generalized LLC-or-Inc version)
3. Below the query, paste the input:
   ```
   Entity Name: DJP3 Consulting Inc
   Entity Type: Inc (Illinois corporation)
   State: Illinois
   Property Address: [leave blank or use D.J.'s business address if appropriate]
   Assessed Value: N/A
   Year Acquired: N/A
   ```
4. **Do not press enter.**

### On-stage paste/click sequence
1. Cmd+Tab to Claude.
2. Deliver the meta-framing line: *"I'm going to unmask MY OWN business entity. Live. In front of all of you. Because I would never put a stranger's name on this screen without their written consent."*
3. Press **Enter**.
4. While Claude runs the trace (~60–90 sec — this is the longest of the five demos because it's genuinely doing research): narrate the steps as they appear. *"Watch — Illinois Secretary of State. Formation filing. Annual report. LinkedIn cross-reference…"*
5. When output lands, scroll to the Decision-Maker Name row.
6. Deliver the comedy beat: *"Yes, that's me, hi."* Pause. Let the laugh land.
7. Read the portfolio signal column (does Claude find any other entities tied to you?)
8. Read the archetype column.
9. Read the suggested opener out loud, verbatim.
10. Cmd+Tab back to slide 21.

### Success markers — THIS IS THE CRITICAL ONE TO VERIFY IN REHEARSAL
- Claude identifies DJP3 Consulting Inc as an Illinois corporation
- Claude surfaces **D.J. Paris** as the officer/principal on the trace
- Claude produces a LinkedIn or web cross-reference link to D.J.
- Claude outputs the full table structure (not just a narrative answer)
- The "How I found them" column cites real source URLs (Illinois SOS portal, LinkedIn)

### Failure modes and recovery — READ CAREFULLY

- **Claude cannot find DJP3 Consulting Inc in the Illinois SOS portal:** This is the single failure mode you must plan for. Before the talk, you must have verified manually that the entity is findable. If during rehearsal Claude struggles, add a manual context anchor to the prompt: *"Note: DJP3 Consulting Inc is filed with the Illinois Secretary of State. Search the ilsos.gov business entity portal directly if needed."* Test this version.
- **Claude finds the entity but cannot name the officer:** Add to the prompt: *"Specifically check the most recent annual report filing for listed officers."* Test.
- **Claude returns a narrative instead of the table:** Follow up with *"return as the table format specified."* This usually fixes it in one shot.
- **Claude hallucinates a different human name:** **CATASTROPHIC.** This is why you rehearse this one the most. If Claude returns a wrong name in rehearsal even once, modify the prompt to anchor your identity more strongly OR cut to the backup screenshot. Do NOT read a wrong name on stage.
- **Complete failure:** Cut to the backup screenshot on slide 20a. Deliver: *"And in the interest of not lying to you, this one is a little slower live — but here is what it returned when I ran it last night. Same output."*

### Rehearsal count
**Minimum 8 successful runs for this demo specifically.** This is your highest-risk demo. If you cannot get 8 clean runs in a row in the week before the talk, fall back to a pre-recorded screen recording played inside the slide. That is not a failure — that is a pro move. A flawless recorded demo beats a failed live demo every time.

### Backup plan escalation ladder
1. Live demo works → do the live demo
2. Live demo flaky but works 3/5 tries → keep live demo, have backup screenshot on standby
3. Live demo works less than 3/5 tries → replace with screen recording inside the slide, narrate over it
4. Nothing works → cut the demo, show the query + a static screenshot, narrate: *"In the interest of time I'm going to show you what this produces rather than run it live."*

## DEMO 5 — BECOME THE AGENT AI RECOMMENDS (GEO)

**Hero slide:** 22–24 | **Demo slide:** 24 | **Budget:** ~240 seconds on screen (longest demo — 3 phases)

### Pre-load
1. Open a fresh Claude conversation, tab-label "HERO 5 — GEO"
2. Paste the full Hero #5 query with your inputs filled in:
   ```
   MY MARKET: Chicago, Illinois
   MY NICHE: Listing agent at Kale Realty, specializing in North Side Chicago neighborhoods including Avondale, Logan Square, Lincoln Park, Lakeview, Lincoln Square, and Roscoe Village
   MY CURRENT VISIBLE ASSETS: Keeping It Real podcast (host), Kale Realty agent, tapthis.co prompt library, Instagram @delfin.paris, [add: any Inman/HousingWire press, any books, any other public assets]
   ```
3. **Critical:** run this query the morning of the talk to see what Claude currently says about the Chicago agent landscape. You need to know in advance whether you're named in Phase 1.
4. **Do not press enter** for the stage run.

### On-stage paste/click sequence
1. Cmd+Tab to Claude.
2. Deliver the vulnerability line: *"I'm going to run this on MY market. Live. Because I want you to see that this works — and I want you to see what Claude actually says about the agents in my city, including whether it mentions me."*
3. Press **Enter**.
4. While Phase 1 runs (~60–90 sec): narrate *"Phase 1 — baseline. Who does Claude think the top Chicago agents are right now?"*
5. When Phase 1 lands, read the agent names out loud.
6. **The reaction beat depends on whether you are in the list:**
   - If you ARE named: *"And I don't say this to brag, I say this to show you that this is repeatable and you can do it too. Here's why Claude cited me…"* then move to Phase 2.
   - If you are NOT named: *"And look — I'm not in this list. Yet. And that's the whole point of this query. Because now I know exactly what I'm missing."* This is the stronger version, ironically. Don't fake it if you're named — but if you're not, lean all the way in.
7. Scroll to Phase 2. Read one agent's authority signals row aloud.
8. Scroll to Phase 3. Read the 90-day hero asset out loud.
9. Read one of the three 30-day content-piece titles out loud.
10. Deliver the CTA bridge: *"That right there — Phase 3 — is the agenda of the AI strategy call I offer. Literally. This is what we build together."*
11. Cmd+Tab to slide 25.

### Success markers
- Phase 1 produces a list of at least 3 Chicago agents by name
- Phase 2 produces a table with authority signals and sources for at least 3 agents
- Phase 3 produces: What's Working / What's Missing / 90-day hero asset / 12-month playbook / 3 content piece titles
- Claude does NOT recommend "post more on Instagram" — the constraint should prevent this

### Failure modes and recovery
- **Phase 1 names zero real agents or clearly hallucinated ones:** This happens if Claude's web access is flaky. Cut to backup screenshot. Say *"In rehearsal last night I got a much richer answer — here's what it returned…"*
- **Claude names you but for the wrong reason:** Good, roll with it. *"See — Claude thinks I'm known for X. That might or might not be true. This is the baseline, and from here I can decide what to reinforce."*
- **Phase 3 output is generic:** Follow up: *"Give me three SPECIFIC content piece titles that a Chicago consumer would type into ChatGPT."* Re-run.
- **Total failure:** Backup screenshot.

### Rehearsal count
**Minimum 5 successful runs.** Because Claude's web-retrieved answers can drift day to day, run this the morning of the talk to see the current baseline.

## Universal recovery principles

1. **Never show a spinning wheel for more than 20 seconds.** Cut to backup.
2. **Never read a hallucinated name out loud.** Ever. If you see a name you can't verify, say *"and you'll notice Claude is surfacing some names I'd want to verify before I acted on — that verification step is where agents get in trouble."*
3. **Never apologize for the AI.** The audience doesn't care about the AI's feelings, they care about whether you look in control.
4. **Your speaker energy covers a 5-second delay.** Your speaker energy does not cover a 60-second delay. Know the difference.
5. **Every demo has a backup screenshot on the next slide.** Every one. No exceptions.
6. **The backup screenshot is the real demo.** The live run is the theater. If you internalize that, you will never panic.

When something breaks, your first move is already queued.
